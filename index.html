A DICHOTOMY OF LOS ANGELES / HOMELESSNESS IN THE CITY OF ANGELS

Homeless population in the city of Los Angeles increased from roughly 8,700 in 2010 to nearly 15,000 in 2013. Of that, more than 8,300 are unsheltered. According to the most recent data available from the LA Homeless Services Authority, a number of factors have contributed to the increase.

Some of the factors include the economy, which has had a negative impact on jobs and wages, along with the fact that more and more families are staying single and living in cars or tents rather than in affordable housing. Another problem is that people who are homeless have fewer resources available. People in a homeless situation are less likely to have a job or a social safety net.
